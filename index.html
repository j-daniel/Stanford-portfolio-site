Experience

Design and Development

This past summer, I worked at DNAnexus as a front-end engineering intern, where I worked with a small team to re-implement their global-scale genomic data management platoform using React.js, Redux, atomic design, and functional programming. I am currently working with the Bejerano Lab at the Stanford Medical School to design and implement an interface for AMELIE, a diagnostic tool for clinical geneticists. I am currently working on a quarter-long class project designing Notable, a notetaking tool for students with audio and visual disabilities based on Stanford d.school principles, and used similar principles to help design an award-winning team submission to the Stanford health++ hackathon.


Teaching

I have held four positions as a teaching assistant or section leader at Stanford. The first was a three-week summer intensive virology course called Viruses in the News, taught by professor Bob Siegel through the Sophomore College program. I have also been a section leader for CS 106A: Programming Methodology twice, and am currently a course assistant for CS 109: Probability and Statistics for Computer Scientists. Responsibilities for these positions have included holding office hours, grading assignments and exams, providing individualized feedback to students, and assisting professors in planning course material and logistics.


Health and Public Policy

While interning in the Global Health Policy Center at the Center for Strategic and International Studies in Washington, D.C., I tracked American response efforts during the Zika crisis in early 2016, attending briefings on Capitol Hill and assembling daily and weekly updates as the situation unfolded. As a Stanford in Government Fellow at the Marin County Department of Health and Human Services, I learned about policy implementation at the local level as I assessed the feasibility of a new county-wide program to expedite and fund hiring unemployed public benefits recipients into local government offices.
